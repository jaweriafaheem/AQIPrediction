Explainable AI: Model Weights & SHAP

SHAP Feature Importance Values (Tomorrow Model)

Key Insights Breakdown

1. PM2.5 Lags (Past air quality)
Previous days' particulate loads represent the baseline weight of the models. Stagnant weather patterns and high yesterday readings strongly project high tomorrow ratings.
2. Wind Speed (Atmospheric Clearing)
Wind speed acts as a negative predictor. Faster breezes successfully flush away exhaust fumes and sweep dry dust particles, indicating a negative SHAP score (lowering predicted AQI).
3. Temperature & Humidity
Relative humidity levels and ambient temperatures alter chemical reactions of secondary particulate formations, contributing to seasonal variation cycles.
